About the Author

Elena Montague is an award-winning author whose literary fiction has captivated readers and critics alike. With a background in anthropology and journalism, she brings a unique depth of research and emotional resonance to her narratives.

Her novels have been translated into 18 languages and have received numerous accolades, including the prestigious Penrose Literary Award and two National Book Award nominations. Elena's work explores themes of memory, identity, and the intricate connections between people and places.

When not writing, Elena lectures at literary festivals worldwide and mentors emerging writers through her nonprofit organization, The Narrative Foundation.
