Between Fear and Courage

Published: October 4, 2023

The Difficult Period of one Kid named Marven who found himself in front of a hard decision and difficult obstacles in his young age, will he allows his thoughts to consume him or will he fight them?

Don’t judge me, I will be clear with you, I’m the villain here but what made that person is my heroism. I will explain everything in this short novel, bring you a dark coffee and biscuits, It is going to be a long story, not a funny one but a real one.

Chapter 6: The Art Of Making Mistakes

We are not here to understand life, it's complicated and our brains can't handle this truth. The life that can make you a billionaire in one hour is the same that can make you homeless in half an hour. We have to accept this fact and we have to deal with it. However, every person has his own life, he starts it with crying and ends it with people crying over him. Tears follow us wherever we go, whether we are happy or sad. Life can be hard sometimes, for everyone, poor or rich, old or young. We are living on the same planet but every one of us has his own story, a story that you can narrate either proudly or with tears but we need to have the feeling of responsibility for every moment in our life, we are the writers of our own book, we all have chapters inside those books that we are not proud of, chapters that we all will skip while telling our story but those chapters are the ones who taught you a lot, who changed you, maybe to the better or the worse, but for sure it will change you depending on how you take it in the first place.

Chapter 11: Silent Betrayal

A lie doesn’t just break a bond between two people—it fractures the very essence of the person who trusted. To live in a lie is to unknowingly walk in darkness, believing the light you see is real until one day the truth blinds you. It’s not just the betrayal that hurts, it’s the realization that every moment, every feeling, was built on a foundation that never existed. When trust is broken, it’s not only the relationship that falls apart but the person who gave their heart to what they thought was the truth. The deepest wound lies in discovering that the life you were living, the love you believed in, was an illusion. A lie doesn’t just steal trust—it steals reality, leaving you to question everything you once knew. In the end, the pain of the lie isn’t just in the deceit, but in the way it leaves you unraveling, forcing you to pick up the shattered pieces of who you are. When truth finally comes to light, the person who was lied to is never the same, because they didn’t just lose trust—they lost a part of themselves

Une nuit À Taghazout

Publication date December 9, 2025

Une nuit à Taghazout est un voyage poétique né en une seule nuit, lorsque l’auteur, porté par la mer, le silence et la vérité de son cœur, a laissé ses mots raconter l’amour qu’il vit encore aujourd’hui. Ce n’est pas l’histoire d’une rupture ni d’un manque, mais celle d’un amour réel, présent, solide, qui inspire chaque vers et qui accompagne l’auteur depuis le premier jour jusqu’à maintenant. Dans ces pages, Marwane Lamtaifi capture la beauté d’un sentiment qui grandit, qui traverse les saisons, qui transforme la vie et qui offre un refuge à l’âme. À travers une écriture sincère et profonde, il célèbre la femme qui partage sa route, leur histoire, leurs souvenirs, et ce lien unique qui continue de se renforcer. Une nuit à Taghazout est un hommage à l’amour vécu, à l’amour quotidien, à l’amour vrai : celui qui ne s’éteint pas, celui qui construit, celui qui reste.

Format: Paperback
Language: French
Acheter sur Amazon

Introduction

À tous les amoureux du monde, à tous les hommes qui aiment sans jamais cacher ce qu’ils ressentent, car aimer ouvertement est une force rare, presque sacrée. À ceux qui, malgré la peur, malgré les regards, trouvent encore le courage d’exprimer leurs sentiments, par des mots, des gestes, ou simplement par la façon dont ils regardent quelqu’un. Dans un monde où l’amour porte aujourd’hui tant de visages différents, parfois déformés par le temps, par la douleur ou par les illusions, il existe encore des êtres qui préfèrent dire ce qu’ils ressentent plutôt que de laisser le silence trahir leur cœur. Les écrivains et les poètes, eux, continuent de croire en cette magie fragile ; ils écrivent sur leurs amours, sur leurs vies, sur les battements de cœur qui les hantent et les guérissent à la fois. Vous qui aimez sans honte, vous qui osez dire « je t’aime » quand tant d’autres se taisent, vous êtes la preuve que l’amour n’a jamais perdu sa force. Ce livre vous appartient aussi, à vous qui faites du monde un endroit où les sentiments méritent encore d’être vécus et partagés.

Under the Fallen Moon

Paperback – November 20, 2025

“There are losses that don’t belong to time; they belong to the soul.”

In the ashes of World War II, a German soldier named Löwe survives the battlefield, but not his memories. Haunted by his father’s shadow and the ghosts of men who never came home, he carries a silence that even peace cannot heal. His body may have survived the war, but his heart remains trapped in it. When fate leads him into the hands of Charlotte, a young French medic whose compassion transcends borders, everything he believes about love, loyalty, and forgiveness begins to unravel. She is supposed to hate him, yet she chooses mercy. He is supposed to be her enemy, yet she becomes his only light. Together, they discover a fragile love that dares to exist amid ruin, a love forbidden by nations but sanctified by the moonlight that watches over them. Between the sound of gunfire and the quiet of their stolen moments, they learn that even in the darkest times, tenderness can survive, and that love can be the most courageous act of all. Under the Fallen Moon is not a tale of heroes or victory. It is the story of a man learning to live after surviving too much, of a woman brave enough to heal the one she should despise, and of a love that refuses to die, even when everything else does. A poetic, soul-stirring novel about war, love, loss, and the quiet bravery of the human heart, Marwane Lamtaifi’s luminous prose captures the beauty within tragedy and the eternal struggle to find light in darkness.
