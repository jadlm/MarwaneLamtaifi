Chapter 6: The Art Of Making Mistakes

We are not here to understand life, it’s complicated and our brains can’t handle this truth. The life that can make you a billionaire in one hour is the same that can make you homeless in half an hour. We have to accept this fact and we have to deal with it. However, every person has his own life, he starts it with crying and ends it with people crying over him. Tears follow us wherever we go, whether we are happy or sad. Life can be hard sometimes, for everyone, poor or rich, old or young. We are living on the same planet but every one of us has his own story, a story that you can narrate either proudly or with tears but we need to have the feeling of responsibility for every moment in our life, we are the writers of our own book, we all have chapters inside those books that we are not proud of, chapters that we all will skip while telling our story but those chapters are the ones who taught you a lot, who changed you, maybe to the better or the worse, but for sure it will change you depending on how you take it in the first place.

Prologue: The Missing Twin

They said we shared everything—from our mother's womb to our first breath, from childhood secrets to adolescent dreams. But they never told us we would also share the darkness. When they found Leo's empty bed that morning, the sheets still warm, I knew something was wrong. Not just missing-wrong, but gone-forever wrong.

I stood in the doorway of our shared room, the space between our beds stretching like a canyon. On my nightstand, a photo of us at twelve, arms around each other's shoulders, grinning with missing teeth. On his, nothing. Just the ghost of where his things should have been.

Le Commencement

La mer murmurait des secrets que seul le cœur pouvait comprendre. À Taghazout, sous le ciel étoilé du Maroc, le temps semblait suspendu entre deux respirations. L'air sentait le sel et la liberté, et dans ce silence peuplé de vagues, j'ai trouvé les mots que je cherchais depuis toujours.

Ce n'était pas une histoire de rupture ou de manque, mais une célébration. Une célébration de l'amour qui dure, qui grandit avec les saisons, qui transforme les jours ordinaires en trésors. Chaque vers est né de cette nuit, porté par le vent de l'Atlantique et la vérité d'un cœur qui aime sans réserve.

When You Left, Life Began

They say love leaves slowly,
Like the tide retreating from shore,
But yours left in an instant—
A door closing, a lock turning,
The sudden silence after a symphony.

I searched for you in empty rooms,
In the spaces between words,
In the shadows you left behind.
But you were already gone,
A ghost in my own home.

And in that emptiness,
In that vast, terrifying quiet,
I found something unexpected:
Myself.

Chapter 1: The Last Letter

The moon fell that night. Not literally, of course—the celestial body remained in its proper place—but in every way that mattered to Elena, the moon had crashed to earth. The letter in her hands felt heavier than any weapon she'd seen on the battlefield, the words blurring through tears she refused to shed.

"My dearest Elena," it began, as all his letters did. But this one ended differently. This one ended with "goodbye" instead of "until we meet again." The war had taken many things from her—her home, her family, her innocence—but this loss felt different. This was the theft of a future she'd only dared to imagine in stolen moments between bombings.
